Why PDFs Persist – and Why That’s Misplaced

  1. Authors provide “as‑is” camera‑ready documents.
    – Let AI format from author’s content, preserving style and accuracy.
  2. Belief that PDF is easier or looks more “official.”
    – Responsive design keeps the look but works on any screen.
  3. Flipbook/PDF seen as “country‑club standard.”
    – True standards value readability and respect for readers.
  4. Legacy workflows built for print.
    – Most members read on phones; print‑first no longer fits.
  5. Unaware of WCAG readability standards.
    – WCAG offers clear, proven benchmarks for better communication.
  6. No policy for responsive design or concern for phone use.
    – Lack of policy allowed flipbooks; WCAG‑aligned guidance prevents repeat.
  7. Staff not trained in modern tools.
    – Brief AI‑guided training ensures consistent, simple results.
  8. Cellphone readership is low.
    – Could readership be down because the format drove them off?
  9. No one complains (except you).
    – None of the current staff were here when H&H moved to flipbook; complaints would have surfaced then, not now.
