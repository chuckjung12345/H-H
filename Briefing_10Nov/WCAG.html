WCAG & CPL — The Readability Standard for Digital Communication

  • WCAG has been the accepted web-accessibility benchmark since 2015; major publishers and news organizations follow it.
  • It defines readability rules covering contrast, spacing, and reflow across all devices.
  • For Mariner Sands, the critical metric is Characters Per Line (CPL) — the factor that determines real-world legibility.

Key Point: The phone band (38–48 CPL) and desktop band (60–75 CPL) do not overlap. Their sweet spots (≈42 vs ≈66) are widely separated — no fixed PDF or flipbook can be optimal for both.

WCAG Reflow: Text must wrap without horizontal scrolling at ≤320 px width. PDFs and flipbooks cannot meet this rule.
Result: A desktop‑optimized PDF is never mobile‑compliant.
Solution: Responsive HTML adjusts line length and spacing automatically per device.
